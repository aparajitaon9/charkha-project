Every travel guide to India begins with a variation of the same sentence: *India will overwhelm your senses.* This is true, and also useless as preparation, because there is no way to prepare for it intellectually.

What you can do is understand what you're walking into — structurally, logistically, culturally — before you arrive.

## The basic facts of scale

India is not a country in the way France or Japan is a country. It is a subcontinent containing more linguistic diversity than Europe, more religious diversity than anywhere else on earth, and a range of climates from Himalayan alpine to tropical coastal.

"India" is not one thing. The food in Tamil Nadu bears almost no resemblance to the food in Kashmir. This matters practically: your experience will be determined almost entirely by where in India you go, and in what order.

## What first-timers get wrong

The most common mistake is trying to do too much. India rewards slowness. A week in one city will give you more than two cities in a week.

The second most common mistake is sticking to the Golden Triangle — Delhi, Agra, Jaipur — without context. These are worth seeing, but they are the most touristic parts of India, and they give a particular impression that may not be representative.

## Logistics that actually matter

**Transport:** Train is the backbone of Indian travel. Book on IRCTC as far in advance as possible.

**Money:** ATMs are widely available in cities. Carry cash for smaller towns and local transport.

**SIM card:** Get one at the airport on arrival. Airtel and Jio are the main networks. This will transform your experience.
